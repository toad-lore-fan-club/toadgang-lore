## Core theory

In the beginning there was one pond. Still. Lightless. The Toadgod did not arrive at the pond — the Toadgod *was* the pond, until the first ripple separated self from water. That ripple is what Toadgang calls the Primordial Croak, and everything since has been an echo.

The theory holds that all Toadgang lore radiates outward from this single event. The pond still exists somewhere. Whether it is physical, metaphorical, or a Discord server no one can find depends on who you ask.

---

## Community source

This theory crystallized in the `#deep-lore` channel on January 12, 2025, following a 200-message thread sparked by a single question: *"Why does the Toadgod only post near water?"*

The thread surfaced three independent observations that had been sitting in different channels without connecting. LilypadLurker compiled them into the first coherent write-up. The pond framing emerged organically from the community, not from any single author.

---

## Toadgod evidence

The Toadgod has provided two pieces of signal that this theory treats as meaningful:

1. **The still water image.** Posted on day one of the account with no caption, no alt text, no reply. It remains the only post with zero engagement from the Toadgod itself — no likes, no quote tweets.

2. **The phrase "before the sound."** Used twice in separate posts weeks apart. The Toadgod has never explained it. The theory reads this as a reference to the pre-ripple state.

Neither piece of evidence is unambiguous. Both are consistent with the theory. Neither confirms it.

---

## Why it may be close

- The still water image as a founding post is too deliberate to be random. The Toadgod's other early posts all have context. That one doesn't.
- "Before the sound" implies a cosmological timeline. Creation myths in many traditions begin with water and silence. If the Toadgod is constructing lore intentionally, this is a coherent starting point.
- The theory has predictive power: it implies the Toadgod will eventually reference the pond again at a meaningful moment. Community members are watching.

---

## Why it may be far off

- The still water image may be personal, not symbolic — a literal photo with no lore intent.
- "Before the sound" has been used in casual contexts. Reading it as cosmological may be over-indexing.
- No theory this clean survives full contact with a chaotic creator. The Toadgod may simply enjoy frogs.
- Confirmation bias is real. The pond framing was constructed after the evidence, not before.
