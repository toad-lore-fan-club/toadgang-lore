## Core theory

Almost every Base meme token launches its initial liquidity pool on Uniswap (or its Base-native fork, the default for new ERC-20s). Toby did not — its first pool was seeded on SushiSwap. The theory holds that this was a deliberate signal, not a convenience, and that it encodes the entire TobyWorld endgame: a "same but different" fork that out-competes the incumbent it copied.

The historical rhyme is exact. SushiSwap was created in 2020 by the pseudonymous "Chef Nomi," who forked Uniswap's open-source code line-for-line, then added a token incentive (SUSHI rewards to LPs) and executed the "vampire attack" — draining roughly $840M–$1B in liquidity out of Uniswap within days by promising rewards to anyone who migrated their LP positions. Sushi was, in other words, a copycat launch that became a credible rival by being *the same code but with a different reward mechanic and a different soul.*

If Toadgod chose Sushi liquidity on purpose, the message is: TobyWorld is the Chef Nomi move applied to the next layer. Toby copies the familiar AMM/meme template ("just another frog on Base") but adds proof-of-time mechanics, an eventual native DEX (Satoswap), and a yield flywheel — then vampire-attacks mindshare and liquidity away from the incumbents the same way Sushi did to Uniswap.

## Why it may be close

Three independent facts line up. First, the venue choice is genuinely anomalous — defaulting to Sushi over Uniswap on Base takes deliberate action. Second, Toadgod is a pseudonymous founder running a "fork the familiar, add a soul" playbook, which is structurally identical to Chef Nomi's. Third, the explicit Satoswap roadmap shows the project always intended to build its own DEX — exactly the trajectory Sushi took (fork → token → own venue → multi-chain). A founder this deliberate about numerology and timing is plausibly deliberate about liquidity venue too.

---

## Why it may be far off

Sushi has been live on Base since 2024 and offers community-governed LP rewards and lower-friction pool creation; choosing it could be purely practical and carry no symbolic weight. The "same but different" rhyme may be pure pattern-matching — Sushi's own rebrand slogan and Toby's dual-mint are not causally linked. And the Chef Nomi story has a dark coda (the $13–14M dev-wallet withdrawal, the SBF handover) that a founder who prizes "incorruptible, unruggable" distribution would presumably *not* want to evoke. If anything, Toby's pristine-distribution ethos is the *anti*-Chef-Nomi.
