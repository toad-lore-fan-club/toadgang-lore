## Core theory

Vitalik Buterin's SchellingCoin (2014) proposed a decentralized oracle: participants vote on whether a fact is true, and anyone who votes with the majority earns a reward P. Truth becomes the **Schelling point** — the answer everyone picks because they expect everyone else to pick it. In 2015 Vitalik published "The P + epsilon Attack," showing the scheme is breakable: a briber who credibly commits to pay P+ε to voters on a false answer (but only if the false answer *loses*) makes voting falsely a dominant strategy, capturing the oracle at zero cost. The salvage paths he and later researchers (the ethresear.ch "Bribery Bidding Wars" thread) identified are: (1) pay voters in a **secondary token that loses value if the oracle is shown corrupt**, (2) require **large deposits with escalating slashing** — the more contested the vote, the more is at stake — and (3) the **more parties with a vested interest, the more secure** the oracle becomes.

The theory: TobyWorld is a Schelling oracle built with exactly these mitigations, and **Proof of Time is the deposit/slashing mechanism.** Toby is the base asset; **Patience** is the secondary token you must *not sell* (selling forfeits the entire vault — a slashing condition); holding duration is the reputation/stake weight; and the community is the large vested-party set that makes capture expensive. "Proof of Time" / "Proof of Trust" is the human-readable name for a bribery-resistant Schelling consensus where your vote weight is how long you've held without defecting.

## Why it may be far off

Shared vocabulary is not a shared mechanism. Plenty of staking tokens have lockups and forfeiture without being oracles, and "Proof of Time" may just mean "rewards for holding" with no voting/consensus layer at all. The Vitalik reply is a fan thanking a famous person under his viral post — thousands of accounts did the same; it shows admiration, not collaboration or shared design. Following Vitalik, Szilágyi, and Stark on Farcaster likewise shows interest, not insider involvement — any Ethereum-curious account might follow all three, and Toadgod has never said a word about the Geth dispute itself. Nothing on-chain shows Toby resolving external facts (the defining job of an oracle), and "displace Chainlink" is community ambition with zero supporting artifact. The whole oracle frame may be retrofitted lore.
