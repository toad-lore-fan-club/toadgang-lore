## Core theory

Brian Armstrong has publicly and repeatedly named "flatcoins" as one of the crypto ideas he is most excited about — a stablecoin variant pegged not to the dollar but to **purchasing power / a basket of goods / CPI**, so that one unit buys the same basket today and years from now. This is on the record from Armstrong's 2023 "10 ideas" thread and multiple interviews; Base has been cited as a likely venue for an inflation-resistant asset.

The theory holds that **Satoby** is the flatcoin. The flywheel as the community reconstructs it: hold Toby → it converts/swaps 1:1 into Satoby → Satoby is the stable, basket-pegged unit of account inside TobyWorld, with yield denominated in BTC/cbBTC rather than USD. The "Toby → Satoby" transmutation (blue frog → orange/golden coin) is read as the volatile-asset → stable-purchasing-power transition that defines a flatcoin. Some members (erick.btc, @Based_Toby) extend this to a separately-named unit, **$ONE**, as the actual CPI-pegged payout token.

In this frame, Toby is not "a meme that might list on Coinbase." It is Coinbase's flatcoin R&D shipped through a deniable, fair-launched, anonymous front — a skunkworks flatcoin.

## Why it may be close

The flatcoin thesis is the rare Toby theory anchored to a *named, documented, external* signal: Armstrong has said on the record he wants this product, and Base is the natural home for it. Toby's distribution (70% airdropped to ~1.4M Coinbase-adjacent wallets) is exactly the kind of pre-seeded user base a Coinbase flatcoin would want. And Satoby is confirmed canon by Toadgod's own site, so the *destination token* exists — the only open question is its peg.

---

## Why it may be far off

A flatcoin needs a credible inflation oracle (Truflation-style) and a supply mechanism that mints/burns against CPI — there is zero on-chain or lore evidence Toby has either. Everything Toadgod has actually said points to a **BTC/sats** denomination (Satoby = Satoshi + Toby, yield in cbBTC), which is the *opposite* of a CPI flatcoin — a BTC-pegged asset is volatile by design. The community may be conflating two incompatible theses (BTC-yield vs. CPI-flat). And no Coinbase employee or filing has ever linked Coinbase to Toby; the Armstrong connection is circumstantial (shared Japanese/classical-music trivia with engineer Yuga Cohler, per community Medium posts).
