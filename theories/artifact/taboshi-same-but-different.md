## Core theory

Toby's "Taboshi" exists as two things that share a name but differ in form: **taboshi1** (an ERC-1155 with a large supply, community-logged in the hundreds of millions) and **TABOSHI** (the scarce mint, community-logged around 186k). They look identical at the level of branding and lore, but they are mechanically distinct — different supplies, different tiers, different roles. The community ratio repeatedly cited is **1111 taboshi1 = 1 TABOSHI**.

The theory: this "same name, different token" structure deliberately mirrors the "same but different" framing Sushi used during its 2024–2025 rebrand period (Sushi → Sushi Labs, soft-rebrand to just "Sushi," new product names like Blade/Wara/Susa/Kubo under one identity). The Zora collects circulating in the community (zora.co/collect/base:0x5c0bf08936bccfbb6af24b4648a9fb365caa2f4e tokens #1 and #2) are read as the on-chain expression of the same idea — a pair of mints that are presented as one artifact in two states.

If true, the dual-mint is a Rosetta stone for the whole ecosystem: Toby itself is "same but different" — a meme on the surface, a proof-of-time infrastructure token underneath.

## Why it may be close

The 1111 ratio is not invented by the community — it is one of Toadgod's three published sacred numbers, and it happens to be the exact bridge between the two mints. That is a strong internal-consistency signal. The two-token "foundation tier + scarce tier" pattern is also a well-trodden meme-ecosystem design (SHIB:BONE, the very comparison @Montake1 draws: "Toby:Taboshi mirrors Shib:Bone"), so the structure is real and intentional even if the Sushi gloss is decorative.

## Why it may be far off

The "same but different" link to Sushi is the weakest joint — and browser verification of the source tweet makes it weaker. The actual @SushiSwap post reads "same same, but different. 🍣🥢" and is dated **May 17, 2026**, which is *after* the TABOSHI/taboshi1 launches. So TABOSHI cannot have "mimicked" it; if there is any relationship it runs the other way or is pure coincidence. The SHIB/BONE analogy (@Montake1: "Toby:Taboshi mirrors Shib:Bone") explains the dual-mint completely without invoking Sushi at all. And the supply figures the community cites are inconsistent (492M vs 900M vs 400M for taboshi1), which suggests the mechanics are being reverse-engineered rather than read off a confirmed spec.
